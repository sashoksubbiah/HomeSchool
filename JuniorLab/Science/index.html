üßä States of Matter üí®

üìö Let's Learn!

Everything around you is made of matter! Matter is anything that takes up space. Your toys, water, and even the air you breathe are all matter!

Matter can exist in three main forms called states:

  • üßä SOLID - Has a fixed shape. Particles are packed tightly together and can only vibrate in place. Examples: ice, rocks, your desk!
  • üíß LIQUID - Takes the shape of its container. Particles can slide past each other. Examples: water, juice, milk!
  • üí® GAS - Spreads out to fill any space. Particles move freely and quickly. Examples: air, steam, helium in balloons!

üî• Heat energy can change matter from one state to another! When you heat ice, it melts into water. When you heat water more, it turns into steam!

üå± Living Things ü¶Å

üìö Let's Learn!

Living things are amazing! They can grow, eat, move, and have babies. Let's learn about plants and animals!

üå± Plants are living things that make their own food using sunlight! Here's how a plant grows:

  • 1. Seed - A tiny package with a baby plant inside
  • 2. Sprout - The seed opens and a tiny stem pokes out
  • 3. Plant - Leaves grow and the plant gets bigger
  • 4. Flower/Fruit - The plant makes flowers or fruit with new seeds!

ü¶Å Animals come in many types:

  • Mammals - Have fur and drink mother's milk (dogs, cats, elephants)
  • Birds - Have feathers and most can fly (eagles, penguins)
  • Fish - Live in water and breathe with gills (goldfish, sharks)
  • Reptiles - Have scales and are cold-blooded (snakes, lizards)

‚ö° Forces & Motion üß≤

üìö Let's Learn!

A force is a push or a pull! Forces make things move, stop, or change direction.

üí™ Types of Forces:

  • Push - When you move something away from you (pushing a toy car)
  • Pull - When you move something toward you (pulling a wagon)
  • Gravity - The force that pulls everything down toward Earth (why things fall!)
  • Friction - The force that slows things down (why a ball stops rolling)

üß≤ Magnets are special! They can push and pull without touching!

  • Every magnet has two ends: North (N) and South (S)
  • Opposite poles ATTRACT - N and S pull toward each other!
  • Same poles REPEL - N and N push away from each other!

üåç Earth & Space ‚òÄÔ∏è

üìö Let's Learn!

Our home, Earth, is an amazing planet spinning in space!

üíß The Water Cycle: Water moves around and around!

  • 1. Evaporation - The sun heats water in oceans and lakes, turning it into invisible water vapor that rises up!
  • 2. Condensation - High in the sky, water vapor cools and forms tiny droplets that make clouds!
  • 3. Precipitation - When clouds get heavy, water falls as rain or snow!
  • 4. Collection - Water flows into rivers, lakes, and oceans, and the cycle starts again!

üåç Day and Night: Earth spins like a top! The side facing the Sun has daytime, while the other side has nighttime. One full spin takes 24 hours - that's one day!

üçÇ Seasons: Earth also travels around the Sun in one year. Because Earth is tilted, different parts get more sunlight at different times, creating seasons - Spring, Summer, Fall, and Winter!
